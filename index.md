## Abstract
Large language models (LLMs) are widely em￾ployed for reflective error correction in task planning; however,existing methods heavily rely on self-exploration, rendering them susceptible to erroneous experiences accumulated from ineffective attempts. To overcome these limitations, we pro￾pose the Flexible Instructional Scaffold Reflection Framework (FISRF), an agent-mentor architecture that assesses the diffi￾culty of correction and magnitude of improvement to adaptively select reflection strategies and mentorship when appropriate,thereby efficiently accumulating effective error correction ex￾perience.
